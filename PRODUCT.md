# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Arvin Monie, one person on a self-directed 14-week AI / AI-security certification
sprint (Jun 29 – Oct 2, 2026) on the path to CISO. Uses it on a laptop, mostly in
the evenings after work, for a 2–3 hour study block Monday–Friday. The job to be
done: see what to study today, tick off the exact Coursera modules just finished,
and feel the progress accumulate so the habit holds for fourteen weeks. Plain,
globally-readable English (single-region audience, but written clearly for anyone).

## Product Purpose

A single-file, local-first study tracker. It holds the full plan as a tree —
6 programs → 21 courses → 70 Coursera modules — and lets the user mark progress at
the module level, with completion rolling up to the course and certificate. It
keeps a weekday streak, weekly and total study hours, the current week of the plan,
and a LinkedIn visibility checklist. Success = the user opens it on a weekday,
logs a session, and the streak survives all 14 weeks.

## Brand Personality

Focused, premium, quietly confident. An editorial study journal, not a SOC console.
Three words: deliberate, earned, calm. It should make steady daily work feel
substantial without cheerleading.

## Anti-references

- The generic dark-navy SaaS dashboard with neon-cyan glow and a rainbow of
  category pills (this is what it looked like before, and it reads as AI-default).
- Gamified habit apps that shower confetti and badges for a single checkbox.
- The hero-metric template: four identical gradient stat cards in a row.

## Design Principles

- **One accent, real hierarchy.** A single deep accent carries progress and
  selection; everything else is ink and paper. Distinction comes from type and
  space, not from coloring every element.
- **Progress you can feel.** Module-level granularity so a single evening visibly
  moves the bars; rollup so the big picture stays legible.
- **Honest state.** Show the real numbers. No fake momentum, no inflated streaks.
- **The tool disappears into the task.** Standard affordances, fast, keyboard- and
  screen-reader-friendly; the centerpiece is the plan, not the chrome.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, visible focus rings, real
checkboxes and buttons with labels and `aria-expanded` on accordions. Full
`prefers-reduced-motion` alternative. Plain English throughout.
